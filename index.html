Day 1

✈️ Arrive Madrid

Saturday, May 16
Land early, check in, and ease into Madrid with a market lunch, Retiro Park, and an epic tapas crawl through La Latina.
6:50 AM
Land at Madrid-Barajas T4
Immigration is quick for US passports. Grab bags and head to the taxi rank.
Tip: Flat-rate taxi from T4 to central Madrid is €33.
8:00 AM
Check in at URSO Hotel & Spa
Boutique hotel in Chamberí in a restored 1920s palace. Request early check-in when booking — they're great about it. Drop bags and freshen up.
Tip: If the room isn't ready, they'll store luggage and you can use the spa.
9:30 AM
Breakfast
URSO has a beautiful courtyard breakfast. Or walk to Tomá Café (Calle de la Palma 49) for specialty coffee and pastries — a Madrid institution.
10:30 AM
Explore Malasaña & Chueca
Madrid's most vibrant neighborhoods. Independent shops, street art, café culture. Walk down to Gran Vía and through Plaza de Cibeles.
1:00 PM
Lunch at Mercado de San Miguel
Gourmet market near Plaza Mayor. Graze on Manchego, Ibérico ham, croquetas, and a glass of Verdejo.
Tip: Arrive before 1:30 PM to beat the lunch rush.
3:00 PM
Retiro Park & Prado Area
Rent a rowboat on the lake, see the Crystal Palace. If you've got energy, the Prado is right there. Otherwise just enjoy the park.
5:00 PM
Siesta / Spa
You've been up since you landed. Head back, nap, use the thermal spa circuit. Recharge.
8:30 PM
Vermut at La Venencia
Legendary sherry bar in Huertas that hasn't changed since the 1920s. No photos allowed. Just fino, manzanilla, and oloroso from the barrel.
Tip: Cash only. Order a fino with olives.
9:30 PM
Tapas crawl in La Latina
Calle Cava Baja is Madrid's best tapas street. Hit Juana La Loca (famous tortilla pintxo), Casa Lucas (modern tapas), and Txirimiri (Basque pintxos). Two or three stops with wine at each.

🏨 URSO Hotel & Spa

  • Calle de Mejía Lequerica, 8 · Chamberí
  • Restored 1920s palace · 78 rooms · Full spa
  • ~€220-280/night · Early check-in friendly
  • Check Amex FHR availability for extra perks
Day 2

⚽ Madrid → Seville

Sunday, May 17
High-speed train to Seville, check into the Gran Meliá, and catch Sevilla FC vs Real Madrid at the Sánchez-Pizjuán.
8:00 AM
Breakfast at URSO
Fuel up. Tostada con tomate (classic Spanish toast with grated tomato and olive oil) and strong café con leche.
9:30 AM
Taxi to Atocha Station
Madrid's main AVE hub. The station has a famous indoor tropical garden worth a look while you wait.
10:00 AM
AVE Train → Seville
2 hours 30 minutes of comfortable high-speed rail. WiFi, café car. Book Preferente class for more legroom (~€20 more).
Tip: Book on Renfe.com. Morning trains run frequently.
12:30 PM
Arrive Sevilla-Santa Justa
Quick taxi to your hotel. The station is ~15 minutes from the historic center.
1:00 PM
Check in at Hotel Colón Gran Meliá
Five-star on Plaza Nueva, overlooking the cathedral. Book through Amex FHR for $300 credit, daily breakfast, room upgrade, and late checkout.
1:30 PM
Lunch: Bodega Santa Cruz
Beloved no-frills tapas bar on Calle Rodrigo Caro. Montaditos, fried fish, cheap fino. Perfect pre-match fuel.
3:30 PM
Walk to the Stadium
25-minute walk through Nervión, or quick taxi. Soak up the pre-match atmosphere — Seville's football culture is electric.
5:00 PM
⚽ Sevilla FC vs Real Madrid
La Liga at the 43,000-seat Sánchez-Pizjuán. The atmosphere at a premium fixture like this is incredible. Gol Norte or Preferencia for the best experience.
Tip: Tickets from ~$140 on SeatPick. Buy early — premium fixture.
7:30 PM
Post-match drinks
Cañas (small draft beers) at the bars around the stadium. The neighborhood buzzes after matches.
9:00 PM
Dinner: El Rinconcillo
Seville's oldest bar since 1670. They chalk your tab on the counter. Carrillada (pork cheeks), espinacas con garbanzos, and house wine.

🏨 Hotel Colón Gran Meliá — 3 Nights

  • Canalejas, 1 · Plaza Nueva, Seville
  • Amex FHR: $300 F&B credit · breakfast for 2 · room upgrade · late checkout
  • Rooftop plunge pool · Clarins spa
  • ~€275-350/night via FHR · Use $600 Amex hotel credit
Day 3

🍷🏖️ Sherry & Cádiz Beach

Monday, May 18
Sherry tasting in Jerez, then a long beach afternoon in Cádiz with sunset drinks over the Atlantic. Rent a car for the day.
8:30 AM
Breakfast at the hotel
FHR complimentary breakfast: fresh OJ, Ibérico ham, local pastries, café con leche.
9:30 AM
Drive to Jerez (~1 hr)
Rent a car for the day (~€40) or book a guided tour through La Belle Seville / Viator (~€90-120/pp with tastings).
10:30 AM
Bodegas Lustau
One of Jerez's most respected sherry houses. Tour the solera system with tastings of fino, amontillado, oloroso, and PX. World-class winemaking with centuries of history.
Tip: Book at lustau.es. ~€15-25/person.
12:15 PM
Lunch: Bar Juanito, Jerez
Legendary tapas bar on Calle Pescadería Vieja. Famous alcachofas (artichokes) and chicharrones. Fino sherry from the barrel. Quick, perfect, affordable.
1:15 PM
Drive to Cádiz (30 min)
Europe's oldest continuously inhabited city. The coastal drive is gorgeous.
2:00 PM
Playa de la Caleta — Beach Afternoon
Golden sand flanked by two castles (Santa Catalina and San Sebastián). This is the Bond film beach from Die Another Day. Grab a spot, swim, and soak it in. The water in May is refreshing but swimmable (~67°F). Plenty of time to relax — you've got all afternoon.
Tip: No lounger rentals here — bring towels from the hotel. There are showers on the beach.
4:30 PM
Wander Old Town Cádiz
Rinse off and explore the old town — Plaza de las Flores, the seafront promenade along Campo del Sur, Cádiz Cathedral. The late afternoon light here is golden and gorgeous.
6:00 PM
Sunset drinks: La Clandestina
Rooftop bar with views over the Atlantic. Cocktails or a glass of local white wine while the sun drops into the ocean. One of those moments.
7:30 PM
Drive back to Seville (~1 hr 15 min)
Return the rental car at the hotel or keep it overnight if you want it for Day 4.
9:30 PM
Dinner: Eslava
One of Seville's most acclaimed tapas restaurants in San Lorenzo. The slow-cooked egg with truffle and mushroom is legendary.
Tip: No reservations for the bar. Arrive at 9 PM to snag a spot.
Day 4

🏛️ Seville: Culture & Tapas

Tuesday, May 19
Alcázar, the Cathedral, crossing into Triana, and a vermut-to-flamenco evening.
9:00 AM
Breakfast at the hotel
Leisurely start. Use that FHR credit.
10:00 AM
Real Alcázar of Seville
Breathtaking Mudéjar royal palace — lush gardens, Game of Thrones filming locations. Book timed entry on alcazarsevilla.org. Give yourself 2 hours.
Tip: The upper royal apartments tour (€6 extra) is worth it for smaller crowds.
12:00 PM
Seville Cathedral & Giralda Tower
World's largest Gothic cathedral. Climb the Giralda (originally a minaret) for panoramic views. It's a ramp, not stairs.
1:30 PM
Lunch: La Azotea
Modern Andalusian cuisine. Salmorejo (chilled tomato soup), atún rojo (red tuna). Pair with Manzanilla sherry or Verdejo white.
3:00 PM
Wander Triana
Cross the Guadalquivir to Seville's historic ceramics and flamenco neighborhood. Browse Triana Market for produce, olives, cured meats.
5:00 PM
Pool time at the hotel
Rooftop plunge pool at the Colón Gran Meliá. May in Seville is ~80°F — perfect for an afternoon dip.
7:00 PM
Vermut at Antiguo Bar Abadía
A neighborhood vermutería in Santa Cruz. House-made vermouth on tap, marinated olives, anchovies. This is the wine bar / vermut experience.
8:30 PM
Flamenco (optional)
La Carbonería is free in an old coal yard. Or book Casa de la Memoria for an intimate seated show (~€22).
9:30 PM
Dinner: Casa Robles
Elegant but unpretentious. Excellent seafood, Ibérico pork, and a deep wine list. A proper sit-down for your last Seville night.
Day 5

🚗 Drive to Madrid via Wine Country

Wednesday, May 20
Road trip through the Spanish heartland — Córdoba's Mezquita, La Mancha wine tastings, and Don Quixote's windmill country.
8:30 AM
Breakfast & check out
Use remaining FHR credit at breakfast.
9:00 AM
Pick up rental car
Santa Justa station or hotel area. Mid-size or SUV through SIXT or Europcar. One-way to Madrid is standard (~€60-80 total).
10:30 AM
Córdoba: Mezquita-Cathedral
1.5 hour drive. The famous mosque-cathedral with its forest of red-and-white striped arches — one of Spain's most iconic buildings. Quick coffee in the Jewish Quarter after.
12:30 PM
Drive into La Mancha
North on the A-4 through rolling plains. Windmills, olive groves, and vineyards to the horizon. Quintessentially Spanish.
1:30 PM
Wine tasting in Valdepeñas
Right on the highway. Stop at Bodegas Navarro López or Bodegas Real for Tempranillo (called Cencibel here) and Airén whites. Pair with Manchego cheese.
Tip: Tastings are often free or €5-10.
3:00 PM
Lunch at a roadside venta
Hearty Manchegan cuisine: migas (fried breadcrumbs with chorizo), pisto manchego (Spanish ratatouille), local wine. Don Quixote country.
4:30 PM
Continue to Madrid (~2 hrs)
Straight shot up the A-4.
6:30 PM
Check into URSO Hotel & Spa
Drop the car at a nearby rental return. Use the thermal spa circuit to unwind from the drive.
9:00 PM
Dinner: Sala de Despiece
Buzzy modern tapas in Malasaña. Dishes on butcher paper, menu reads like a meat-cut chart. Creative, delicious, very Madrid.

🚗 Car Rental Details

  • ~530 km (330 mi) · 5 hrs direct, 7-8 hrs with stops
  • ~€60-80 one-way + ~€30-40 fuel · Mostly toll-free via A-4
  • US license is valid for short stays in Spain
  • Drop off at Madrid Atocha or Chamartín station
Day 6

🍇 Ribera del Duero + Gofío

Thursday, May 21
Day trip to Spain's great Tempranillo region — wineries, a castle, roast lamb lunch — then back to Madrid for dinner at Gofío with Anthony & Ang.
8:00 AM
Early breakfast at URSO
Quick start for the drive north.
8:30 AM
Drive to Ribera del Duero (~2 hrs)
North on the A-1 toward Peñafiel. The Duero River valley landscape is beautiful.
10:30 AM
Bodegas Protos, Peñafiel
Iconic Ribera winery built into the hillside beneath Peñafiel Castle. Richard Rogers-designed facility. Taste Crianza, Reserva, and Gran Reserva Tempranillos. The castle above has the Wine Museum.
Tip: Book at bodegasprotos.com. Tours from ~€15.
12:30 PM
Bodegas Emilio Moro
Family-run with beautiful grounds. This is the intimate, Penedès-style winery experience you're looking for. Often includes food pairings.
2:00 PM
Lunch: Aranda de Duero
Famous for lechazo — roast suckling lamb in wood-fired ovens. Try Asador Casa Florencio or Mesón de la Villa. Pair with a local Ribera Reserva. Quintessential Castilian meal.
4:00 PM
Drive back to Madrid (~2 hrs)
Wine in the trunk, memories in the bank.
6:00 PM
Free time / Shopping
Salamanca neighborhood for upscale shopping, or pick up gifts — olive oil, saffron, cerámica tiles.
9:00 PM
🌟 Dinner: Gofío — Table for 4
Your favorite from last time! Michelin-starred Canarian cuisine by Chef Sicilia. Reunite with Anthony & Ang for the big dinner. Book well ahead — this place fills up.
Tip: Reserve at gofio.rest or call +34 915 22 81 05. Mention any dietary needs. Tasting menu ~€85-95/pp.
11:00 PM
Nightcap: Salmon Guru
One of the World's 50 Best Bars, in Huertas. Creative cocktails in a vibrant, artsy space. Perfect way to close out the trip.

📋 Practical Info

🚆 Getting Around

  • Madrid: Metro + walking. Airport taxi flat €33
  • Madrid→Seville: AVE from Atocha (2.5 hrs, ~€50-80/pp)
  • Sherry Triangle: Rental car or guided tour
  • Seville→Madrid: One-way car rental (5-8 hrs with stops)
  • Ribera del Duero: Rental car from Madrid (~2 hrs each way)

🍷 Wine Regions

  • Sherry Triangle (Jerez/Cádiz) — Fino, Manzanilla, Oloroso, PX
  • Montilla-Moriles (Córdoba) — Unfortified sherry-style wines
  • Valdepeñas / La Mancha — Tempranillo, Airén whites
  • Ribera del Duero — World-class Tempranillo (your Penedès equivalent)

🍽️ Dining Tips

  • Lunch: 1:30-3:30 PM · Dinner: 9:00-11:00 PM
  • Tapas bars: No reservations. Stand at the bar
  • Tipping: 5-10% at sit-downs. Round up at tapas bars
  • Vermut hour: 12-2 PM (traditional) or ~7 PM
  • Tapas: ~€15-25/pp · Sit-down: ~€40-70/pp · Gofío: ~€85-95/pp

☀️ Weather & Packing

  • Madrid: 60-77°F (16-25°C), mostly sunny
  • Seville: 65-86°F (18-30°C), hot and sunny
  • Pack: Light layers, walking shoes, sunglasses, sunscreen
  • Dress: Smart casual for restaurants. No shorts at cathedrals
